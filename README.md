# Cybersecurity Portfolio

## About Me

My interest in cybersecurity started from a general curiosity about computers during high school. By the time I entered college, I had decided to pursue cybersecurity as a career path. I graduated with a degree in Computer Science Engineering with a specialization in Information Security from VIT, Vellore, which helped me build a strong foundation in computing, security concepts, and technical problem-solving.

After graduation, I began my professional cybersecurity journey as a SOC Analyst in India, where I gained hands-on experience in monitoring, investigating, and responding to security alerts. I later moved into a SOC Consultant role in Qatar, where I continue to develop my skills in security operations, threat detection, and incident response. I was also awarded the Paller Cybersecurity Scholarship in 2025, through which I am currently continuing my cybersecurity learning and professional development.

## CTF Reflection

Before participating in the National Cyber League Spring 2026 CTF, I had some prior exposure to cybersecurity practice through platforms such as Hack The Box and smaller CTF-style activities. However, this was the first CTF where I invested a significant amount of focused time, effort, and documentation into the process. The experience helped me move beyond casual practice and approach challenges in a more structured and disciplined way.

One of the most valuable parts of the CTF was learning how to handle unfamiliar problems. In several challenges, I did not immediately know the correct tool or method, so I had to slow down, examine the evidence, test different approaches, and learn from each attempt. This helped me improve not only my technical skills, but also my patience, troubleshooting process, and confidence when facing new cybersecurity problems.

Participating in this CTF also reminded me why hands-on practice is important in cybersecurity. Reading about concepts is useful, but applying them under challenge conditions made the learning more practical and memorable. Overall, the NCL experience helped me reconnect with CTFs more seriously and gave me a clearer direction for continuing my cybersecurity learning through SANS, labs, and future competitions.

## CTF Highlights

During the NCL Spring 2026 CTF, I worked across a wide range of cybersecurity categories, including cryptography, forensics, log analysis, network traffic analysis, OSINT, password cracking, scanning and reconnaissance, enumeration, and web application exploitation.

A major highlight of the competition was the breadth of practical exposure. I was able to apply different investigation methods depending on the challenge type, such as analyzing artifacts, reviewing logs, inspecting network activity, identifying encoded or encrypted data, researching open-source information, and validating findings through tool-based analysis.

Another important highlight was developing a more structured problem-solving process. Instead of jumping directly to tools, I learned to first understand the objective, examine the evidence, form a hypothesis, test possible approaches, and document what worked or did not work. This helped me become more patient and methodical when dealing with unfamiliar problems.

I also documented my work privately in a detailed format with challenge overviews, methodology, tools used, observations, and lessons learned. In accordance with NCL rules, this public portfolio does not include challenge walkthroughs, solution steps, flags, or answer details.
